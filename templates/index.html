Click2Hear: Interactive Spatial Audio Separation and Localization using Visual Cues

Avinash Subramaniam*, Debottam Dutta*, Chaitanya Amballa*

Dept. of Electrical and Computer Engineering

University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

* = Equal contribution

Abstract:

We present Click2Hear, an interactive system that utilizes visual cues to generate binaural audio from a video with mono audio and at the same time performs spatial audio separation and sound source localization. We present Click2Hear, an interactive system that utilizes visual cues to generate binaural audio from a video with mono audio and at the same time performs spatial audio separation and sound source localization. Mono audios lack spatial information in itself to localize or separate sources without any external information such as speaker identity and visual cues. Without any speaker or prior source information, most of the existing methods utilize visual information present in the video frames to achieve these tasks. These methods take inspiration from the real world that, humans also have a sense of localizing the sound with the help of visual information they capture. This visual information helps in both separating and localizing sources at the same time. In addition to helping with audio source separation, visual information also can give a sense of the spatial location of the audio source. Existing audio-visual methods attempt these tasks separately or require sequential processing to do both tasks. We investigate the generalizability of a popular existing method in localizing and separating mono sound mixtures to more realistic scenarios by conducting several experiments. Then, we propose a unified framework that does both of the aforementioned tasks with the additional task of binauralization in one shot. Our experiments show promising results towards achieving this task.
